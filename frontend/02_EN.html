Trail 2 - Il Passo di Annibale
From Val di Luce on the Duca street up to Passo d'Annibale
Path does not present technical difficulties (most of the route takes place on a dirt road) and which allows you to arrive in one of the most fascinating points of the Apennines. From Passo d'Annibale you dominate the Val di Luce and you can admire the main peaks: Monte Rondinaio and Monte Giovo on one side and Monte Gomito and Dente della Vecchia on the other form a truly incredible landscape frame. In early autumn, the blueberry groves turn the slopes red, making this walk even more fascinating.

POINT OF INTEREST
Casa Coppi (m. 1357). Casa Coppi, in Val di Luce, a few km from Abetone; going up you reach Foce a Giovo where you can enjoy a spectacular view. The route also passes through the famous Hannibal pass.
Foce di Giovo. From here, go around Borra al Fosso (m.1806) on the right, if you go up the ridge you reach Mount Rondinaio (m.1964) from here you can enjoy an excellent panorama of the Apuan Alps, the Northern Apennines and you can also clearly see Lake Baccio and Lake Santo.
Pass of Hannibal. According to some scholars this pass could have been the one crossed by the Carthaginian leader in his encirclement march towards Rome, even if the most probable hypothesis remains that of the Calanca Pass. From the Pass, near the stone building, it is possible to reach the Balzo delle Rose, an excellent panoramic point, in a few tens of minutes.
Val di Luce or Val di Pozze can be easily reached by taking the SS. 12 which from Abetone descends towards Modena.
